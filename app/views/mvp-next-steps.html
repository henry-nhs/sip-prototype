You have not applied for the Test and Trace Support Payment yet. To do this, you will need to complete the following steps.

{{step3Label}}

NHS Test and Trace will send you an email and text message.

Emails and texts are sent between 7.30am and 8:30pm, and can take up to an hour to come through. If you submitted your details after 8:30pm, you will receive the email and text the following morning.

If you cannot find the email, you should check your junk mail or spam folder.

" }) }}

Before you can apply for the payment, you will need to:

  • follow the link to enter some personal details
  • take note of your NHS Test and Trace Account ID, which you will need for the next step

{{step4Label}}

After you have completed the steps above, you should receive a second email or text message from NHS Test and Trace. When this has arrived, you can apply for the payment from your local authority.

You can find the application form on your local authority’s website by using this link: https://www.gov.uk/test-and-trace-support-payment. Make a note of this so you can return to it later.

As part of your application, you will need to enter your NHS Test and Trace Account ID. This is the 8-digit number included in the email and text message from NHS Test and Trace. Your local authority may call this something different, such as an account number or CTAS number.

They will ask you for information, including:

  • proof of your identity
  • proof of benefits you’ve received
  • your employment details (including National Insurance number)
  • your bank account details

Continue to self-isolate

You must not leave your home until the end of your self-isolation period.

NHS Test and Trace may contact you while you’re self-isolating to check in and offer support.

You do not need to take a test unless you have coronavirus symptoms. You can check what the coronavirus symptoms are.
