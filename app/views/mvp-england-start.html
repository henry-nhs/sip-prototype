You can use this service if the NHS COVID-19 app has detected that you have been in contact with a person who has coronavirus. This means you are advised to self-isolate.

If you are on a low income, are unable to work from home and will lose income as a result of self-isolating, you may be entitled to a payment of £500 from your local authority under the Test and Trace Support Payment scheme.

Before you start

You cannot claim this payment more than once within a self-isolation period. If you are asked to self-isolate for overlapping periods of time, this counts as one self-isolation period.

As part of the application process, you will register with NHS Test and Trace. When you do this, you will be legally required to self-isolate. If you do not comply, you may face a penalty of at least £1,000.

The app cannot be used to track you, and the personal details you provide to this service will not be held anywhere in the app.

Check your eligibility

To be eligible for a Test and Trace Support Payment, you must:

  • be employed or self-employed and unable to work from home
  • have lost income as a result of having to stay at home
  • receive at least one of the qualifying benefits below

Qualifying benefits, include:

  • Universal Credit
  • Working Tax Credit
  • Income-based Employment and Support Allowance
  • Income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Income Support
  • Housing Benefit
  • Pension Credit

If you don’t currently receive a qualifying benefit, you may be eligible for a discretionary benefit from your local authority.
